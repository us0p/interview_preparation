KISS (Keep It Simple, Stupid)

Is a design principle that suggests simplicity should be a key goal in 
design, development, and other fields, advocating for straightforward 
and uncomplicated solutions over unnecessarily complex ones.

Implications:
- Simplicity.
- Clarity.
- Efficiency.
- Usability.
- Flexbility.
- Risk reduction.

Kiss in Software Development:
- Maintainability.
- Debugging.
- Scalabitliy.
- Performance.
- Reduced Technical Debt.

How to KISS:
- Identify core objectives.
- Focus on essentials.
- Simplify Design and workflow.
- Prioritize clarity and undertandability.
- Iterate and refine.
- Use simple tools and techniques.
